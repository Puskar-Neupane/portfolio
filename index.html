About Me

I am an Electrical Engineer with a deep passion for integrating renewable energy sources into existing power grids. My work focuses on solving challenges in grid integration, power electronics, and the application of Machine Learning and AI in electrical engineering. I have a strong academic and professional background in power systems and have worked on innovative projects that address real-world problems in this field.

Projects

  • Virtual Oscillator Control for Inverters (Apr. 2023 – Mar. 2024)
    Designed a VOC control system for a three-phase inverter and operated multiple of them in parallel. Analyzed the behaviors of a VOC (GFM) and PV (GFL) connected microgrid. Compared the performance of the VOC-integrated microgrid against conventional droop control.
  • Forecasting Losses in Magnetic Materials (Apr. 2023 – Dec. 2024)
    Completed as part of IEEE PELS-Google-Enphase-Princeton MagNet Challenge 2023. Prediction of loss based on magnetic field, temperature, and frequency of operation. Used LSTM to train the model in the frequency domain.
  • Fabrication of Low-Cost LaApSi Model (Sep. 2023 – Dec. 2023)
    Successfully created and produced a cost-effective laparoscopic appendicectomy simulation model designed specifically for surgical trainees in low and middle-income countries (LMICs).
  • Autonomous Driving Vehicle (Aug. 2022 – Mar. 2023)
    Demonstrated an operational prototype of a self-driving RC car using ANN, CNN, and LSTM architectures for model training, with an article published in a national journal.
